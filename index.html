Australia Road Fatalities Since 1989





Fatalities by Time and Day of Week

There is a clear correlation between weekday and the time of the accident. The least fatal hours are from midnight to 7am from Monday to Thursday as there is most likely very few drivers on the road at this time. The exception to this is on the weekends, where people are likely to stay out later, potentially under the influence. The most fatal hours are around 3pm to 6pm, most likely due to the roads being the busiest around these times. The number of fatalities begins to decrease from 8pm onwards, except on Fridays and Saturdays where fatality rates continue to stay high until around 3am.

Fatalities in By State and Date

There is a clear trend of the number of fatalities decreasing over the years since 1989, which may be as a result of many different factors, such as better technology, laws and regulations or education.

The main contributors to fatal road accidents are NSW, Queensland and Victoria, likely due to their larger population relative to the other states. Fatalities by Time of Day and State

Most of the fatalities of road accidents seem to come from NSW, Queensland and Victoria.. As mentioned, this is most likely due to these states being the most populated states of Australia, resulting in a higher likelihood of road accidents and death.

While the fatalities seem to be evenly split between day and night, there does seem to be a slight skew towards night time. We should also consider that there are less drivers during the night time, meaning the accidents to drivers ratio would be much higher at night, signifying drivers as being more dangerous.

Fatalities by Time of Day and Speed Limit

The number of fatalities at each speed limit seems to increase as the speed limit increases, suggesting that high speed is one of the major influencing factors when it comes to fatal accidents. While some of the higher speed limits do have relatively lower fatalities, such as 75km and 90km, this is more likely due to these speed limits being less common.

During the night accidents at around the 60km speed limit seems to result in more fatalities when compared to its day time counterpart, while during the day the difference between fatalities at 60km and fatalities at 100km is larger.

Across the States

Let's take a closer look at the total number of fatalities in every Australian state. It seems that relative to other states, ACT, Victoria and NSW seems to have the highest fatality to road length ratio, with the highest ratio of 136 fatalities per 1000km of road belonging to ACT. However, these same states are on the lower end when it comes to the fatalities to population ratio, with Northern Territory now having the highest ratio of 68 fatalities for every 10 000 people living in the state.
